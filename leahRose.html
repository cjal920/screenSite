LEAH ROSE

NEW YORK CITY

SUMMER 1929

She has it all. Youth. Beauty. A handsome and successful lawyer husband. A lavish Upper East Side penthouse. And all the money in the world.

And during the last summer of the 1920s, the long hot days Leah Rose spends with husband Alfred and their circle of fabulous friends indulging in all the delights the good life has to offer seem to go on without end.

But what Leah and no one else around her knows is that these glorious golden days are numbered. She doesn't know that just a few miles south of her exclusive Upper East Side address, a storm is brewing down on Wall Street. A storm born of reckless speculation that will strike at the heart of the global economy in the final days of October. And when it strikes, it leaves her cherished world of privilege and security utterly destroyed.

And while Alfred leaves town to try to restore that lost world in a risky bootlegging venture, in the desperate months that follow the most catastrophic financial crisis of all time, Leah will have to fight to make her way in this harrowing new one.

Staying with a trusted but strapped friend from Alfred's youth in a squalid house in Greenwich Village he shares with other starving artists, intellectuals and people on the fringes of society, Leah will face new challenges her pampered existence has left her ill-equipped to meet - an unplanned pregnancy; a landlord who wants her out unless she can pay rent; having to find a job to do it; and with each passing day he's gone, the growing fear that Alfred isn't coming back.

But as a consequence of her arduous journey and the people she will meet along the way, Leah will reconnect with her Jewish heritage, awaken to the plight of the powerless, and gain a far greater sense of purpose than her comfortabe but ultimately hollow old life ever afforded her. Yet even as the spectre of dangerous new political ideologies begin to gain legitimacy in a world growing more and more desperate to relieve the burdens of the global economic depression engulphing it, we can take some comfort that strength and wisdom Leah acquired on her journey of self-discovery will not abandoned her as the world hurtles toward the second great war of the century.
